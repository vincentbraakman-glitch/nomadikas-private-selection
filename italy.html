Two homes in the sun-baked heel of Italy. One a 16th-century masseria above the Adriatic near Carovigno — golden stone, walled gardens, an infinity pool reaching for the horizon. Carovigno · Puglia · Adriatic coast

Masseria Belvedere

a 16th-century farmhouse, restored in golden stone

Carved from Puglian stone in the 1500s and meticulously restored in 2021, a working farmhouse that learned to dress in linen. Eight en-suite bedrooms across two floors, sixteen guests at most. An infinity pool reaches for the horizon in the walled garden; a plunge pool and hot tub crown the rooftop terrace. A hammam in the old stone, a wood-fired kitchen under the loggia, an olive grove old enough to have outlived empires.

Villa Acquamarina

a clifftop villa where Italy runs out of land

At the very tip of the Salento, inside the protected coast of Otranto, Villa Acquamarina stands on the cliff with a 180° sweep of the Adriatic. Seven bedrooms, fifteen guests, two sea-view dipping pools cut into the terrace. A jacuzzi and a sauna, a spa for in-villa massage, a wine cellar, a private chef — and, when the evening asks for it, live music arranged on the terrace above the water. Bohemian-chic and barefoot, with the sea on every side.
