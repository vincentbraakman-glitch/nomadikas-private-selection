Spain · Menorca · Two farms

An island of farmers
no one has heard of.

Menorca is the quietest of the Balearics — a biosphere reserve where the land is mostly worked by hand. Both homes are working farm estates: one built around a 14th-century watchtower with its own chapel and a private path to a cove; the other a 19th-century hilltop finca on 60 hectares. The rarest currency on the Mediterranean — privacy, all of it yours.

An aerial view of the estate — the 14th-century watchtower, the 1789 chapel, and 108 hectares of olive grove rolling toward the sea.

Es Bec d'Aguila

a 19th-century hilltop finca · eleven en-suites · 60 hectares all yours

The Eagle's Beak — 19th-century merchant family's hilltop hideaway between Alaior and Es Migjorn. 1,000m² across three levels: French garden with fountain, green-marble bar and library, eleven en-suite bedrooms, 25m pool, gym, yoga studio overlooking the olives, cinema room, ceramic workshop. Farmer Xisco brings the eggs and honey.
