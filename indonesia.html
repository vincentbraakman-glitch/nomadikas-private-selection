The headland.
A week at the edge of the map.

On the wild south-west coast of Sumba — a short flight beyond Bali, then an hour through the hills — a five-acre headland sits nearly surrounded by the sea. We chose this single estate, alone, because it holds what almost nowhere else can: complete privacy, a celebrated off-grid spa and a legendary wave next door, and a whole island that still keeps its own ancient world.

The estate on its sacred headland — five villas, four pools and a hilltop pavilion above 180° of ocean, the surf line breaking just out of frame.
